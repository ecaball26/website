Weekend Routines and Prioritizing the Tasks that Make Me Feel Alive

~My Perfect Schedule as An ADHD Adult~

If you know me, you would know that I loveeee to work on the weekends. As someone that can often get overstimulated but also loves to work on many projects, I can often get distracted by the small tasks that pop-up through the week. So I love working on the weekends when it's quieter. I know, I know, working on the weekends sounds terrible. I'm fortunate enough to love what I would do (I would still do this if I was the richest person in world). However, I've found it a little hard to catalyze any productivity when I try to use my weekday schedule. So this morning, I gave myself the space to breathe and just knit. I had to remind myself and say "you work on the weekends to get ahead not because you're required to." After I finally relaxed into my knitting I found myself happy and in Gen-Z terms "not ready to off myself" at the thought of working on a Sunday.

So that's my main takeaway: on the weekends, I need to schedule my mornings so that I've blocked time off for the things that make feel alive (e.g. knitting, reading at a cafe, going on a trail run, gardening, etc) and then I can saunter or skip into the office in the afternoons.
